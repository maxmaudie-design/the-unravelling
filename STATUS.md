## WHAT THIS PROJECT IS

A long-form essay (currently ~11,200 words) tracing 4,000 years of social contract breakdowns and resets, written to understand contemporary political fracture. Directed by Max Maudie, former journalist, Alberta, Canada. AI collaboration (Claude) is transparent and will be part of the story.

**Publication plan:** Serialized on Substack with "director's commentary" showing the AI collaboration process. Personal framing: "Why I Stopped Trolling Alberta Separatists (And What I Learned About Social Collapse)."

**Core argument:** The "tribalism" and institutional collapse we're experiencing is what happens when a social contract dies and nothing replaces it. Meaningful resets only occur when horizontal friction (groups blaming each other) turns vertical (collective pressure on the system itself).

**The credit model:** The symphony conductor. Max didn't write the individual parts (the scholars did) and didn't play them (the AI did), but conducted the performance into a coherent whole. "Directed by" is accurate and honest.

## INTELLECTUAL LINEAGE â€” HORIZONTAL/VERTICAL FRICTION

The essay's central framework is a synthesis, not an original discovery. Full lineage:

- **Piven & Cloward, *Poor People's Movements* (1977)** â€” closest scholarly precedent; empirically demonstrates when movements succeed (disruptive vertical pressure on elites) vs. fail (fragmentation, institutionalization, horizontal dissipation)
- **Labour organizing tradition** â€” "don't let the bosses divide us"; the idea that worker solidarity requires resisting horizontal conflict (race, craft, region) is as old as the labour movement itself
- **Marxist theory of false consciousness** â€” the idea that the working class is manipulated into identifying with ideological constructs (nationalism, religion, race) rather than their material class interests; horizontal misdirection as a feature of capitalist ideology
- **"Divide and conquer"** â€” ancient political strategy; documented from Roman military practice onward; the structural version of what the essay describes
- **Lateral oppression literature** â€” from anti-racism and decolonization scholarship; describes how oppressed groups internalize and reproduce oppression horizontally against each other rather than directing anger at the source

The essay should explicitly acknowledge this lineage rather than presenting the framework as novel. Piven & Cloward are the primary citation. The synthesis across these traditions is the contribution â€” not the underlying observation.
